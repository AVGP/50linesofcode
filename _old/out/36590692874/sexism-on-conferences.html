Sexism on conferences

November 26th 2012

#Sexism on conferences

To admit this: I have been sexist on my own by just not realizing that I was.

Thanks to my wonderful girlfriend, I learnt to see the mistakes I made in the past - thanks to her!

Tech conferences are still struggling to get out of their sexist "boys only" image.

I guess the only real way around this is doing blind reviews on call-for-papers to make sure no discrimination can happen. But I guess, thanks to the image of the tech-conference scene, the problem kicks in earlier at the moment: There are too few women applying for being a speaker. In that case: Read this wonderful article.

An idea that I don't find helpful (in fact, I think it's sexist) is offering discount on tickets for women. Why should that help?

Because women can't afford the tickets, because they're female?

No.

Do they need additional incentive, because they're just not so interested in tech?

Hell, no!

I think the only ways of making the conferences more appealing are:

  • Clearly communicate, that discrimination, sexism and harassment will be prosecuted
  • Have a code of conduct, that includes penalties for misbehaviour
  • Have people, who make sure, the code of conduct is followed and who actively and passively enforce the proper behaviour of every attendee.

I guess these may be good steps forward re-shaping the conference culture to get sexism, harassment and discrimination out of the tech scene.
