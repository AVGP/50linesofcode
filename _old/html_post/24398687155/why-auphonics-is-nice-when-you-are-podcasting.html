Why auphonics is nice when you are podcasting

2012-06-04 11:47:00 GMT

I recently started a podcast (in German) and was overwhelmed by the resonance I got for it.

My first experience with podcasting was: Audacity crashed a few times randomly and recording podcasts with it was not really enjoyable. I ditched it and recorded it with a simpler audiorecorder. 

That, in turn, made it an extra step to normalize and denoise the sound file. I decided to leave out those post-production steps. But listeners noticed a low volume and some noise in the recording then.

Thankfully, @smnkrl pointed me to Auphonic. It allows to upload a bunch of audio formats and does denoise and normalization automatically, if you wish it to.

It offers even more than just that (which is the baseline for me):

  • Upload of cover image
  • Splitting on chapters and chapter marks
  • Choose the output format and bitrate
  • Filtering and adaptive leveling
  • Upload to SFTP, FTP or Dropbox after production.

And it’s all free.

To me the service seems to be pretty reliable and works perfect. This way, podcast production works like a charm for me.
